What is Social Engineering?

Social engineering is a tactic that people use to obtain unauthorized information. This could be done with several different attacks, but the main goal is to try to get their target to trust them to be willing to give them their perrsonal information.

Types of Social Engineering Attacks

There are several types of social engineering attacks, including:

  • Phishing
  • Baiting
  • Pretexting
  • Quid Pro Quo
  • Tailgating
  • Watering Hole

Examples of Social Engineering Attacks

Here are some examples of social engineering attacks:

  • Pretending to be a higher up person like a CEO or Director sending a phishing link to employees of the company to give them personal information.
  • Getting someone to download hardware onto their devices which allows the attacker to get personal file from the device without them knowing.
  • Calling somoneone pretneding to be their bank provider to resolve an issue and ask for personal information.

How to Protect Yourself from Social Engineering Attacks

Here are some tips for protecting yourself from social engineering attacks:

  • Be aware with phishing links through emails to get their victims to change their password, update some personal information, etc.
  • Rejects any requests you have receive for technical support, personal bank accounts not having sufficient funds etc.
  • Update devices that you use regularly that may contain personal data that could be vulnerable to attackers if software is not up to date.
  • double checking to see if you're talk to the person they say they are. If you recieve a call fromone that clains they work at the bank you use. Give them a call yourself to make sure the issue is actually there or even better physcailly going to the bank.

How Social engineering attacks are executed

  • Attackers usually pretend to be someone that is high up in a company requesting for a password change, money for something, etc.
  • Attackers also target people pretneding to be someone who works at a bank and requesting for money, personal information, etc.
